Factors Affecting Coffee Taste

If the coffee is Under-Extracted, it can develop a Overpowering Sour Taste, Thin Flavor, and Salty Taste.

Your coffee will be Under-Extracted if you don't brew long enough, the water temperature isn't hot enough, you grind too coarsely, or you don't use enough water.


If the coffee is Over-Extracted, it can develop a Overpowering Bitterness, Dull and Empty Flavor, and Dry Sandpapery Mouthfeel.

Your coffee will Over-Extracted if you brew for too long, the water temperature is too hot, you grind too fine, you use too much water.

Grind & Insert

Grind & Insert Coffee

Grind roasted coffee beans and put them in the dripper.

If you grind finely, the amount of body will increase, but if it is too thin, the soft texture will disappear and it will become lumpy and sticky.
If you grind too coarsely, it will be under-extracted.
